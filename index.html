how this sea works
Why sail?
Money buys upgrades, upgrades open water, water holds fish, and fish make friends of strangers. But the long game is above: befriend the islands, fill the codex, witness the wonders — and see what the sea does about it.

Fishing
Fish swim as dark shadows in the fishing grounds — the bright turquoise shallows around islands, the green kelp beds, the coral reefs, and (with a Longline) the dark Deeps. Drift up slowly — a fast wake scatters them. Within reach, press E to cast, then hit Space when the needle crosses the glowing arc. Rarer fish fight harder.

Money
Fish sell at any dock market — fresher is worth more (the Ice Box helps). Delivery contracts pay reliably, with a bonus if you arrive before dusk. Money buys the shipwright's nine upgrades at Hearthquay, and repairs when you've kissed a rock.

Conditions
Time of day and weather decide what's biting. Some fish want dawn, some want rain on the night sea, one wants a starless overcast midnight in the deepest water. The codex (C) keeps your hints.

The hull
Rocks and hard landings chip the hull. Below 30% she slows; at zero she limps. Any dock patches her up for a few coins. Nothing you carry is ever lost.
